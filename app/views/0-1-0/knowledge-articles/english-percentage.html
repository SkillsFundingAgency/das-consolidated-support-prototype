English percentage

Updated 9 November 2017.

English percentage is the amount of your organisation’s payroll that gets paid to employees who live in England.

HMRC works out your organisation’s English percentage using payroll information.

Changes to your English percentage

Your organisation’s English percentage can change if any of your employees’ living situations change, for example:

  • it can increase if your employees who lived in other parts of the UK move to England
  • it can decrease if you hire new employees who live in Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland

HMRC uses information it already has about where your employees live. It’s important that you tell HMRC if any of your employees’ home addresses change.

You can view your latest English percentage in your apprentice service account.

How we work out the levy

To work out the levy, HMRC multiplies the levy declaration by the English percentage. They do this 3 times a year in January, April and October.

Example

Acme Limited declared £500,000 of apprenticeship levy in the tax year April 2017 to March 2018. 90% of Acme Limited’s employees live in England, so their English percentage is 90%.

HMRC multiplies £500,000 by 90% which equals £450,000. They then add £45,000 (a 10% top-up bonus) to the levy funds.

Acme Limited's total levy fund is £495,000.
