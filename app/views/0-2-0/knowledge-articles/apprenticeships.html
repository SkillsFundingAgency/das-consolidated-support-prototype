Apprenticeships

An apprenticeship is a job that combines practical training with off-the-job training and study.

An apprentice will:

  • work with experienced staff
  • learn job-specific skills
  • earn a wage and get holiday pay
  • be given time to study (either 20% or one day a week)

Apprenticeship levels

All apprenticeships lead to industry recognised standards or qualifications. Some apprenticeships have an assessment at the end of the programme to check the apprentice’s ability and skills in their role.

Wages

The national minimum wage (NMW) for apprentices under the age of 19 or in the first year of their apprenticeship is £3.90 per hour. Apprentices who are at least 25 years old and not in the first year of their apprenticeship are entitled to £8.21 per hour.

This is the minimum wage you must pay by law, but most employers pay more.

Working hours

Most apprentices must work at least 30 hours a week, including at least 20% of their time for off-the-job training. The apprenticeship must be completed within working hours.

Your apprentice may work less hours if they have a disability or work as a carer.

Who can apply

You can become an apprentice if you are at least 16 years old and live in England.

Check what role-specific requirements you need when you find an apprenticeship. You can get email and text notifications when a new role is available.

View more information about apprenticeships and employers.

Training providers

Training providers give off-the-job training, assess progress towards qualifications and offer general support to apprentices. They also work with employers to make sure each apprentice gets:

  • an induction programme
  • a training plan (including on-the-job training)
  • regular process reviews
  • opportunities to put training into practice
  • mentoring and support throughout the apprenticeship

The provider, employer and apprentice must confirm that this information is within the apprenticeship agreement commitment statement.

Get support

You can get financial support if you’re a care leaver aged 16 to 24. This is a £1,000 bursary that you’ll get in the first year of the apprenticeship.

If you need help applying for an apprenticeship, you can:

  • speak to us using web-chat
  • email nationalhelpdesk@findapprenticeship.service.gov.uk
  • call us on 0800 100 900
