How does the English percentage affect my levy credit?

Updated 9 November 2017.

HMRC works out the English percentage for each PAYE scheme by dividing the total year-to-date pay bill of employees who live in England by the total year-to-date pay bill of all employees.

This example shows how HMRC uses the English percentage to calculate the levy:

  • employer declares £1000 as a levy payment to HMRC.
  • the employer has an English percentage of 75%.

The levy declaration (£1000) is multiplied by the employer’s English percentage (75%), which gives £750. The government tops up that £750 with an additional 10% (£75) to give an overall monthly credit of £825.
